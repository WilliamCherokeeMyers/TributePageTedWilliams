Ted Williams

The greatest hitter of all time

Here's a time line of Ted Williams' career:

  • 1936 - Ted originally signed to play with the San Diego Padres of the Pacific Coast League
  • 1937 - The Red Sox buy Ted's contract from The Padres
  • 1938 - Ted makes his MLB debut
  • 1941 - Ted leads the American League with a batting average of .406
  • 1942 - Ted wins the American League Triple Crown
  • 1943-45 - Ted was a pilot in the United States Marine Corps
  • 1946 - Williams was named the AL MVP. The Red Sox slugger led Boston to its first AL pennant in 28 years by hitting .342 with 38 homers, 123 RBI and league-leading totals in runs (142), total bases (343) and walks (156)
  • 1966 - Ted was elected to the Hall of Fame

Ted Williams finished his career with a .344 batting average, 2,265 hits, 521 home runs, and 1,839 runs batted in.
